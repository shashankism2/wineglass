How to Calculate the Perimeter of a Square

A square is one of the most fundamental shapes in geometry, characterized by four equal sides and four right angles. One of the simplest yet most important measurements related to a square is its perimeter. Whether you're a student, a teacher, or just someone needing quick geometry help, this guide will teach you everything about how to calculate the perimeter of a square—plus give you an easy tool: our Perimeter of a Square Calculator!

What is the Perimeter of a Square?

The perimeter of a square refers to the total length of all four sides. Since all the sides of a square are equal in length, calculating its perimeter is extremely straightforward.

Formula to Calculate Perimeter of a Square

The standard formula to find the perimeter of a square is:

Perimeter = 4 × Side

Where:

  • Side is the length of one side of the square.
  • The result gives the perimeter in the same unit as the side (e.g., cm, m, inches).

Diagram of a Square

Step-by-Step Examples

Example 1: Side = 6 cm

Formula:
Perimeter = 4 × 6 = 24 cm

Answer: The perimeter is 24 cm.

Example 2: Side = 12.5 meters

Formula:
Perimeter = 4 × 12.5 = 50 meters

Answer: The perimeter is 50 meters.

Example 3: Side = 8 inches

Formula:
Perimeter = 4 × 8 = 32 inches

Answer: The perimeter is 32 inches.
