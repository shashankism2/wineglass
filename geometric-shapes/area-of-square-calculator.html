How to Calculate the Area of a Square

The area of a square is one of the most straightforward calculations in geometry. Whether you're working on a school assignment, home improvement project, or just brushing up on your math skills, understanding how to calculate the area is essential—and incredibly simple once you know the formula.

📐 What Is a Square?

A square is a special type of quadrilateral where:

  • All four sides are equal in length
  • All four angles are right angles (90°)

Because of this symmetry, calculating the area of a square is much easier than other shapes.

✅ Area of a Square Formula

To calculate the area of a square, use this formula:

Area = side × side
or
Area = side²

Where:

  • side is the length of one side of the square
  • area is the total surface inside the square, measured in square units (e.g., cm², m², in²)

🔢 Example Calculations

Let’s go through a few different examples to see how the formula works in real life:

🧮 Example 1: Small Square

Side length = 4 inches
Area = 4 × 4 = 16 in²
✅ The area of the square is 16 square inches.

🧮 Example 2: Large Square

Side length = 12 feet
Area = 12 × 12 = 144 ft²
✅ The area of the square is 144 square feet.

🧮 Example 3: Metric Units

Side length = 3.5 meters
Area = 3.5 × 3.5 = 12.25 m²
✅ The area of the square is 12.25 square meters.

🧮 Example 4: Decimal Input

Side length = 7.2 cm
Area = 7.2 × 7.2 = 51.84 cm²
✅ The area of the square is 51.84 square centimeters.

🧮 Example 5: Very Large Area

Side length = 100 meters
Area = 100 × 100 = 10,000 m²
✅ The area of the square is 10,000 square meters—great for estimating large plots of land!
