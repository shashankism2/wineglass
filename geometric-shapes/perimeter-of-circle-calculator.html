Perimeter of a Circle Calculator


What is the Perimeter of a Circle?

The perimeter of a circle, also called the circumference, is the total distance around the boundary of the circle. If you walk along the edge of a circular object, the distance you travel is its perimeter.

This is a fundamental concept in geometry and has applications in real-world scenarios such as construction, engineering, and measurement.

Formula to Calculate the Perimeter (Circumference)

There are two common formulas depending on the known input:

  • Using Radius (r): Perimeter = 2 × π × r
  • Using Diameter (d): Perimeter = π × d

Where π (pi) ≈ 3.1416, r is the radius, and d is the diameter (2 × radius).

Examples

Example 1: Using Radius

Question: What is the perimeter of a circle with a radius of 7 cm?

Solution:

Perimeter = 2 × π × r
          = 2 × 3.1416 × 7
          ≈ 43.982 cm

Answer: The perimeter is approximately 43.98 cm.

Example 2: Using Diameter

Question: What is the perimeter of a circle with a diameter of 10 meters?

Solution:

        Perimeter = π × d
          = 3.1416 × 10
          ≈ 31.416 m

Answer: The perimeter is approximately 31.42 meters.
