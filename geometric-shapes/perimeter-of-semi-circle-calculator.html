How to Calculate the Area of a Semi-Circle

The semi-circle is a fundamental geometric shape often encountered in real-world applications like architecture, design, and engineering. Calculating its area is useful for estimating space, materials, and design layout. In this article, you’ll learn how to calculate the area of a semi-circle using a simple formula, visualize its parts with an SVG diagram, go through step-by-step examples, and use our free Area of a Semi-Circle Calculator.

What is a Semi-Circle?

A semi-circle is exactly half of a full circle. If you cut a circle along its diameter, you get two equal semi-circles. It has a curved edge (half the circumference of a full circle) and a straight edge (the diameter).

  • The radius (r) is the distance from the center to the edge.
  • The diameter (d) is twice the radius: d = 2r.

Formula to Calculate Area of a Semi-Circle

The area of a full circle is given by:

Area of full circle = π × r²

Therefore, the area of a semi-circle is half that:

Area of semi-circle = (1/2) × π × r²

Where:

  • r = radius of the semi-circle
  • π (pi) ≈ 3.14159

Step-by-Step Examples

Example 1:

Given: Radius r = 7 cm

Solution:

Area = (1/2) × π × r²  
     = (1/2) × 3.1416 × (7 × 7)  
     = 0.5 × 3.1416 × 49  
     ≈ 76.96 cm²

Answer: 76.96 cm²

Example 2:

Given: Radius r = 10 meters

Solution:

Area = (1/2) × π × 10²  
     = 0.5 × 3.1416 × 100  
     = 157.08 m²

Answer: 157.08 m²

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use diameter instead of radius?

Yes! Since r = d / 2, substitute this into the formula:

Area = (1/2) × π × (d / 2)²

Is the curved part counted in the area?

Yes, the area includes everything enclosed by the curved edge and the diameter line — it is half the full circle.

What is the unit of area?

The area is always expressed in square units, such as cm², m², in², etc.
