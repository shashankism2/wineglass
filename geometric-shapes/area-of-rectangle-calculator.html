What Is a Rectangle?

A rectangle is a four-sided polygon (also called a quadrilateral) where:

  • All angles are right angles (90 degrees)
  • Opposite sides are equal in length

Key properties:

  • Length (ℓ): The longer side of the rectangle
  • Width (w): The shorter side of the rectangle (also called breadth)

Formula to Calculate the Area of a Rectangle

The area of a rectangle is the amount of space it covers on a flat surface. It is always expressed in square units, such as cm², m², ft², etc.

Formula:

Area = Length × Width
A = ℓ × w

Where:

  • A is the area
  • ℓ is the length
  • w is the width

Example Calculations

Example 1: Area in Centimeters

Given:

  • Length = 8 cm
  • Width = 5 cm

Calculation:

A = 8 × 5 = 40 cm²

Result: Area = 40 square centimeters

Example 2: Area in Meters

Given:

  • Length = 4.5 meters
  • Width = 3 meters

Calculation:

A = 4.5 × 3 = 13.5 m²

Result: Area = 13.5 square meters

Example 3: Area in Feet (Using Fractions)

Given:

  • Length = 10.5 ft
  • Width = 6.25 ft

Calculation:

A = 10.5 × 6.25 = 65.625 ft²

Result: Area = 65.625 square feet
