How to Calculate the Perimeter of a Rectangle

The perimeter of a rectangle is a fundamental concept in geometry that refers to the total length around the rectangle. Whether you're designing a garden, framing a picture, or coding a geometry calculator, understanding how to calculate the perimeter is essential. What is a Rectangle?

A rectangle is a quadrilateral with four right angles (90°) and opposite sides that are equal in length. It has two dimensions: length (l) and Breadth (b). These two values determine the perimeter and area of the shape.

Perimeter of a Rectangle Formula

The perimeter (P) of a rectangle is calculated using this simple formula:

P = 2 × (Length + Breadth)

That means you add the length and Breadth together and then multiply the result by 2. This accounts for both pairs of equal sides.

Mathematical Notation:

P = 2 × (l + b)

Where:

  • P = Perimeter
  • l = Length of the rectangle
  • b = Breadth of the rectangle
